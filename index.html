Enveseur delivered its strongest year of growth in recent memory, with net revenue rising from €4.64M to €5.36M — an increase of €712K. Order volumes grew at an even faster rate of +17.4%, reflecting an expanding B2B customer base across Europe. Critically, this growth was achieved while maintaining a gross profit margin of 74.9%, demonstrating the inherent scalability of the business model.

In operational terms: the business fulfilled an average of 283 orders per working day, generating approximately €21,400 in daily net revenue — every day, consistently, across 26 active markets.

Enveseur serves a predominantly B2B customer base across Europe, with returning customers representing 68% of total revenue. The high average order value from returning customers — €134.78 — reflects the B2B nature of the business and the repeat purchasing behaviour of businesses managing ongoing packaging requirements.

New customer acquisition accelerated significantly during the year, with new customer orders growing +24% — a leading indicator of future revenue as new customers transition to repeat purchasing. Both segments grew consistently throughout the year, with no quarter showing contraction in either cohort.

Customer cohort health: returning customers placed 26,866 orders at an average value of €134.78, with gross profit margins of 72.3%. New customers placed 46,110 orders — more than the total order count from both segments in the prior year — demonstrating the scale of customer acquisition during the period.

The business operates a distributed European fulfilment infrastructure with warehouses in the United Kingdom, Spain and Slovakia, enabling efficient delivery across all 26 active markets. Customer-facing operations run through more than 30 localised Shopify storefronts, each configured for local language, currency and product range.

Delivery income grew +29.5% to €616K in the year, reflecting the increased volume of orders and the efficiency of fulfilment operations at scale. This infrastructure — built and refined over several years — represents a significant operational asset that supports continued growth without proportional increases in fixed cost.

Scale in perspective: 48.1 million individual units shipped across 26 countries from three warehouses, fulfilling 283 orders every working day. The logistics infrastructure is proven, scalable, and already in place.
